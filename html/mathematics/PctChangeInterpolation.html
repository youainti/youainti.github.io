Interpolation of compounding percentage changes

Let the following define the terms. - X is the final value - P is the principle value - R is the observed percentage change between X and R - n is the number of periods to interpolate. - r is the interpolated rate of change.

Then by definition R = \frac{X-P}{P} \\ \frac{X}{P} = 1 + R

From high school, recall that the compounding formula is: X = P(1+r)^n

Thus by dividing P over, substitution, and algebra we get r = (1+R)^\frac{1}{n} - 1

Application notes

Note that this is different from the standard APR = \frac{R}{n} rate, with n=12.
